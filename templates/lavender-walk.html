Tonie’s Lavender Walk: LGBT History Tour of Dublin

Long considered an indispensable part of Dublin Pride, the LGBTI history walking tour of Dublin returns this June, hosted by its originator, Tonie Walsh.
Tours depart daily from Dublin Castle (pedestrian entrance) in groups limited to a maximum of 15 and with a fully vaccinated host.
Approx. two hour’s duration, there will be a pit stop at the National Photographic Archive, where Living With Pride: Photographs by Christopher Robson is currently showing.

Your Host, Tonie Walsh

Archivist, activist, writer and historian, Tonie Walsh, has had a long engagement with Ireland’s LGBT civil rights movement and social scene since 1979. He is the co-founder of GCN magazine, which he edited during its first two years. In 1997, he re-organised the archive holding of the National LGBT Federation into what would later become known as the Irish Queer Archive (IQA). The archive was transferred to state ownership in 2008. He is currently co-curating the Living With Pride programme at the National Library of Ireland.
